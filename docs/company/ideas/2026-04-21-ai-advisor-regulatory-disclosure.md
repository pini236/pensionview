## The idea

Add mandatory regulatory disclosures to the AI advisor so it cannot be construed as unlicensed investment advice under Israeli law.

## Why it matters

The AI advisor system prompt at `app/api/advisor/chat/route.ts:78` instructs the model: "Never say you're an LLM or mention Claude. You ARE PensionView's advisor." The chat header calls it "יועץ פנסיה" (Pension Advisor) with no disclaimer.

This is a direct exposure under the **Regulation of Investment Advice, Investment Marketing and Portfolio Management Law, 5755-1995** (חוק הסדרת העיסוק בייעוץ השקעות, בשיווק השקעות ובניהול תיקי השקעות). Pension advice is explicitly in scope. Presenting an automated system as a human advisor providing personal financial guidance — without disclosing its automated nature — is exactly what regulators treat as unlicensed advisory activity. The רשות שוק ההון has enforced on fintech products for far subtler violations.

The suggestions the advisor surfaces ("When can I retire?", "Are my fees high?") are not neutral data queries — they invite and deliver retirement planning conclusions grounded in the user's actual financial position. That is the definition of personal investment advice under the law.

Two compounding risks:
1. The "Never say you're an LLM" instruction actively prevents the model from correcting a member who believes they are talking to a licensed human advisor. That suppression is intentional and makes the exposure worse.
2. The monthly insight generated by `lib/pipeline/insight.ts:48` uses the prompt "You are a financial advisor writing a brief monthly summary" — same framing, same gap, but stored persistently in `report_insights`.

Why now: the advisor is already live and being used. Every day without a disclosure is a day of regulatory exposure. The fix is a single-screen UI change plus a one-line system prompt edit — this is the cheapest possible risk mitigation for one of the highest-severity exposures in the product.

## How it might work

- Add a persistent disclosure banner inside the chat panel (above the first message, not dismissible on first visit): "יועץ זה הוא כלי מידע אוטומטי ואינו מהווה ייעוץ השקעות, ייעוץ פנסיוני או שיווק השקעות כהגדרתם בחוק. אין להסתמך עליו כתחליף לייעוץ מורשה." (This advisor is an automated information tool and does not constitute investment advice, pension advice, or investment marketing as defined by law. Do not rely on it as a substitute for licensed advice.)
- Remove "Never say you're an LLM or mention Claude" from the system prompt. Replace with: "You are an automated data tool, not a licensed advisor. If a user asks whether you are a human or a licensed advisor, say clearly that you are not."
- Rename the UI label from "יועץ פנסיה" to "עוזר נתונים" or "מידע פנסיוני" (data assistant / pension information) — the word "יועץ" (advisor) is legally loaded.
- Add a matching disclaimer footer to the monthly insight card on the dashboard: one line, small text, always visible below the insight.
- Update `lib/pipeline/insight.ts` system prompt to remove "financial advisor" framing.

## What we don't know

- Whether the exact disclosure text satisfies the רשות שוק ההון's expectations or whether we need a more specific legal review (escalation trigger: if legal counsel is needed, this becomes Out-of-scope escalation).
- How much the rename from "יועץ" to a neutral label affects engagement — design-lead should weigh in on phrasing that is warm but legally safe.
- Whether we need to add a terms-of-service acceptance gate (separate pitch) before the disclaimer alone is sufficient.
